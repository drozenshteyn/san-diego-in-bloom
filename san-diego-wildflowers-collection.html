Wildflowers

San Diego is home to a remarkable variety of native wildflowers, making it a dream for nature lovers and photographers alike. From coastal trails to inland canyons, spring brings a colorful transformation across the region — but wildflowers can be found blooming in every season if you know where to look.

This collection highlights the quiet beauty of wildflowers throughout the county, from rare blossoms like Chinese houses (*Collinsia heterophylla*) to golden California poppies, vibrant lupines, and delicate rockroses. Whether you're out with a camera or simply taking a peaceful walk, these are some of my favorite places to explore for wildflower photography and to experience the joy of seasonal blooms in their natural habitats:

  • Mission Trails Regional Park
  • Blue Sky Ecological Reserve
  • Lake Poway
  • Lake Murray
  • San Elijo Lagoon Ecological Reserve
  • Silverwood Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve
  • Cabrillo National Monument

Each image in this gallery is a quiet celebration of San Diego’s native flora — captured with care and a deep appreciation for the delicate wonders that bloom all around us.
